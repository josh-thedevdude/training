Soft Deletes:
It ensures that the data is not lost and can be recovered later in a given time.
It is required to enhance user experience in cases where they come back to restore their data.
For example a user can be given the choice to deactive an account instead of permanently deleting before a certain period to allow restoring the account again.
Historical data should also be maintained to allow future predictions, support legal work. 

Hard Deletes:
Permanently deletes the data. Can be restored only if a backup is maintained.
All the data that does not promote to the business logic deserves to be hard deleted like otp codes, session tokens, Caches. 
Old logs and monitoring data, spam messages, fake accounts needs to be hard deleted.
